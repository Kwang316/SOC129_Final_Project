An Analysis of Social Forces and Construction in Family Depictions in Media

Introduction

An Analysis of Family in Arcane and Of Mice and Men

This cinema examines two portrayals of family in media—a classical novel, Of Mice and Men by John Steinbeck, and a modern television series, Arcane. I will examine the role of class, race, ableism, and the social constructions of family, parenting, and marriage. Furthermore, it will examine how varying depictions of families in media can conflict with the common value of the nuclear family as well as implications for how similar examples in the real world are affected by legislation that supports a different view of family.

Sociological Imagination
C. Wright Mills
Social Construction
A Societal Product

Foundational Concepts

It is vital to first define the terms sociological imagination and social construction. Sociological imagination refers to the contextualization of individual troubles--ie. single employment, individual difficult in paying healthcare biill--to more broader and systemic plights on society--ie. employment crisis, healthcare problems. Social constructions refers to the emergence of entities originated by the consensus of a public rather than that of a natural origin, such as money or title. It does not mean that this entity is not useful or should be treated necessarily as entirely subjective, but that its essential origin is not an objective universal origin. These terms will be useful in examination of roles of social forces and constructions in the establishment of family.

Arcane
Structural Force: Class
Piltover vs. The Undercity
A Tale of Two Cities

Structural Forces: Class (Arcane)

Powder and Vi are two blood-related orphaned sisters raised in the economically disadvantaged and crime-ridden Undercity in the fictional landscape of Piltover. Though the landscape and setting of the world is fictional, the elements of the city reflect much of the geographical manifestation of economic disparity. Moreover, the world of Arcane evidently does not have a stable Child Tax Credit policy for the citizens of the Undercity, which possibly could have relieved the difficulty experienced by Vi and Powder, likely qualifiers of subjects of child policy (PBS NewHour, Child Poverty Increases Sharply Following Expiration of Expanded Tax Credit)

Of Mice and Men
Structural Force: Class
The Great Depression
Economic Precarity

Structural Forces: Class (OMAM)

The effects of class are the permeating factor in the circumstance of Lennie Small, George Milton, and the farm’s altercation. The novel takes place during the height of the Great Depression and Dust Bowl, an evident precipice of economic struggle pervasively, but particularly in the emergence of migrant workers, including Lennie and George. In this regard, the establishment of class and the difference between employer and employee is deepened, especially in ties to family. In a traditional nuclear family, the confliction of parental care and economic need as obligated by the cultural values and governmental absence of influence would likely be heightened during periods of economic turmoil. Similarly, in the familial bond of Lennie and George, George is conflicted between economic difficulty and taking care of Lennie, although they are both employed for work. This phenomenon is described in Maia Green & Victoria Lawson’s Recentring care: interrogating the commodification of care, in which they observe the once sentimental and personal quality of care is mutating into a commercialized product subject to marketability akin to a commodity due to the imputation of both economic agency and responsibility of care to families.

Arcane
Structural Force: Gender
Vi & Jinx
Challenging Gender Roles

Structural Forces: Gender (Arcane)

In regards to the construction of gender, Vi and Powder grow up without a mother, challenging common ideals of the nuclear family with a hometaking mother and breadwinning father. In regards to the establishment of the traditional nuclear family, gender roles are commonly assigned with cultual precedence, in regards to the establishment of the family. Due to social and cultual consensus, maternal leave has given more support than paternal leave (Issacs, 2017). In the scenario for Vi and Powder, their custody under a single father would not merit qualifications for such benefits if they existed.

Of Mice and Men
Intersectionality
Crooks, Lennie & Curley's Wife
Marginalized Identities

Structural Forces: Race, Ableism, and Gender (OMAM)

Of Mice and Men uses its supporting characters to show how multiple structural forces intersect. Crooks is isolated by the structural force of racism. Lennie is made vulnerable by societal ableism that has no place for his intellectual disability. Curley's wife, the sole female character, is completely isolated by her gender, not even granted a name. Her tragic fate is a direct result of her powerlessness within a hyper-masculine world that denies her identity, agency, and treats her as property.

Of Mice and Men
Challenging Family Definitions
"I got you an' you got me"
A Bond of Care

Social Construction: Family (OMAM)

In the presentation of family in the context of George and Lennie, family can be viewed as a social construction, where as the nuclear family as described by Meadow and Stacy (2006) is popularized at least in the west due to historical developments particularly in the 20th century, the establishment of George. George states, ‘Guys like us have no family. They make a little stake and then they blow it in. They ain't got nobody in the worl' that gives a hoot in hell about 'em”. Yet, George tells this to Lennie in his final moments, an ironic and somber notice that this is not true about George and Lennie, because as Lennie states, "Because I got you an … "An' I got you. We got each other, that's what, that gives a hoot in hell about us”. Thus, there is an establishment of a strong familial bond or the closest can could currently be attained, but still does not fit in the common formula of a nuclear family.

Arcane
Social Construction: Family
The Found Family
Vander's Care

Social Construction: Family (Arcane)

After being orphaned and taken under the guardianship of Vander, Vi and Powder are integrated into a family with their adoptive brothers Mylo and Claggor. Vander takes the role of an adoptive father, taking custody of Vi, Powder, Mylo, and Claggor. Furthermore, the establishment of family is one of an adoptional nature, with it assumed that none of the adopted children of Vander are biologically related to him. In this sense, the relation of non-biological children as considered as directly family is to some individuals accepted as more appropriate, with individuals of step-families gradually omitting the prefix of step-family (NPR. 2011)

Of Mice and Men
Social Construction: Parenting
George as a Parental Figure
Responsibility and Care

Social Construction: Parenting (OMAM)

Although George and Lennie’s relationship is strongly characterized as fraternal, Lennie’s strong dependence on George does provide George with the need to assume a near parental role. George has knowledge that he would be able to perform better financially without Lennie, stating ‘"If I was alone I could live so easy. I could get a job an' not have no mess”. yet remains with him because of his selfless familial bond. George must continually remind and reprimand Lennie of proper behavior, such as reminding him not to drink from unclean water. Lennie states he continually gives him ‘hell’, akin to relationship of a parent disciplining their child. Thus, like a parent, George takes care of Lennie without expecting anything in return purely out of a familial affection, although they do not resemble the traditional nuclear family model.

Arcane
Social Construction: Parenting
Vander vs. Silco
A Duality of Fatherhood

Social Construction: Parenting (Arcane)

In the aftermath of violent conflict, Vi and Powder are left orphaned at a young age, with Vander becoming their guardian and father figure. Vander is not shown to have evidence of marriage nor is engaged romantically with any person. Thus, in this example, the establishment of the nuclear family is not exemplified. Furthermore, after another incident, Vi and Powder are separated, with Powder being taken under the guardianship of Vander’s former ‘brother’ Silco. Once again, Silco takes an adoptive fatherly role over Powder. Furthermore, after another series of events, Powder is left alone and meets Isha, who becomes similar to a mother or older sister figure. In all these relationships, only Vi and Powder are blood-related, with no common abidement of a traditional nuclear family structure. Yet, there is a strong familial tie to the individuals, with each referring to each other in familial terms.

Conclusion

Redefining the Family Unit

The depiction of the uncommon archetypes of family in Arcane through a single father adoptive family and in Of Mice and Men through a fraternal and paternal role of George with Lennie demonstrates the departure from common ideals of family in the nuclear family structure. It also shows discrepancies in certain policies and legislation such as the Child Policy Act, support for paternal leave, and other benefits attributed to families that individuals similar to the structure of the families examined in these media would possibly not be qualified for reception. Thus, these depictions help to interpose inquiry into how social constructions of family may require reassessment as well as how such varying defintions may affect policy. A political stance on family policy presupposes a sociological defintion of family--one that the depictions provided invites serious questions for inclusivity.
